About Me

I build systems that sit at the intersection of robotics, embedded hardware, wireless telemetry, medical devices, and human-centered design.

Background

I am a Rice University undergraduate studying Electrical and Computer Engineering with a specialization in Neuroengineering and minors in Neuroscience, Engineering Design, and Asian Studies.

My interests include neuroengineering, neural interfaces, medical devices, medical robotics, wireless telemetry, embedded systems, probabilistic modeling, and applied machine learning.

I am especially drawn to projects that require integration across hardware, software, sensing, fabrication, and real-world constraints.

Core Areas

  • Robotics and systems integration
  • Embedded systems and motor control
  • Wireless telemetry and measurement
  • Medical and neurotechnology applications
  • Fabrication, prototyping, and design communication

Technical Focus

Across projects, I frequently work with ROS2, OpenCV, TensorFlow/PyTorch, C/C++, Python, MATLAB, Zephyr RTOS, Git, KiCad, SolidWorks, Onshape, and hands-on fabrication workflows.
