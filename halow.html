Wi-Fi HaLow Telemetry

Research on secure, low-power Wi-Fi HaLow and BLE telemetry for implantable neurological and neurotechnology-relevant systems.

Project Overview

This project explores whether IEEE 802.11ah (Wi-Fi HaLow) can serve as a viable telemetry option for neurotechnology applications, especially where energy efficiency, range, throughput, and implant-relevant constraints matter.

The work includes experimental characterization, protocol comparison, and system-level tradeoff analysis.

Focus Areas

  • Low-power wireless telemetry
  • BLE vs Wi-Fi HaLow comparison
  • Power and energy-per-bit measurement
  • Implant-relevant communication constraints

My Role

I work on telemetry experimentation, firmware-related support, measurement infrastructure, and broader system evaluation. That includes analyzing throughput/power tradeoffs and connecting these measurements to neurotechnology use cases.
